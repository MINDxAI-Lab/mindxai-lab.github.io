  <p class="lead">
    The MINDxAI Lab shared the first paper kicking off its National Science Foundation EDSE project at the Applied Human Factors and Ergonomics International Conference 2025.
  </p>

  <section class="paper-card">
    <h2>A Systematic Review of Ground Truth Labeling and Prediction for Cognitive Workload Adaptive Systems</h2>
    <p>
      This paper provides a foundation for the lab's NSF EDSE project with Dr. David Kaber, with contributions from master's student Udit Kumar Das and Ph.D. student Moajjem Hossain Chowdhury.
    </p>
    <h3>Key Takeaways</h3>
    <ul class="takeaway-list">
      <li>Most studies still rely on retrospective self-report for ground-truth workload labeling.</li>
      <li>Predictive models perform well within subject, but performance drops across users.</li>
      <li>Only 9% of published work evaluates a real-time closed-loop adaptive system, highlighting a major research gap.</li>
    </ul>

    <a class="paper-link" href="https://openaccess.cms-conferences.org/publications/book/978-1-964867-75-5/article/978-1-964867-75-5_71" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Read the paper</a>
  </section>
