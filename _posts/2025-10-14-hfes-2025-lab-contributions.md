  <p class="lead">
    The MINDxAI Lab contributed one lecture and two posters at the ASPIRE HFES 2025 Annual Meeting, spanning the Surface Transportation and Healthcare tracks.
  </p>

  <div class="contribution-grid">
    <section class="contribution-card">
      <h2>Lecture | Surface Transportation Track</h2>
      <h3>Impact of Aggressive Driving on Road Safety: A Systematic Review</h3>
      <p>
        This systematic review integrates diverse research on aggressive driving into a unified, multi-stage framework linking psychological, behavioral, and systemic factors shaping road safety.
      </p>
      <ul class="session-details">
        <li><strong>Location:</strong> Grand Hall J</li>
        <li><strong>Date and Time:</strong> October 14, 3:40-4:00 PM CDT</li>
        <li><strong>Student Lead:</strong> Yan Chen</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="contribution-card">
      <h2>Poster | Healthcare Track</h2>
      <h3>Understanding Communication Barriers in Healthcare: A Review</h3>
      <p>
        This work examines how linguistic, cultural, psychological, and cognitive factors jointly shape communication failures in healthcare and how adaptive human-AI systems can enhance patient-centered care.
      </p>
      <ul class="session-details">
        <li><strong>Location:</strong> Riverside East</li>
        <li><strong>Date and Time:</strong> Wednesday, October 15, 5:30-6:30 PM CDT</li>
        <li><strong>Student Lead:</strong> Moajjem Hossain Chowdhury</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="contribution-card">
      <h2>Poster | Undergraduate Special Highlight</h2>
      <h3>An Analysis of Operational Processes in Coffee Shops Aimed at Improving Baristas' Response to Highly-Customized Orders</h3>
      <p>
        This senior-student project from ISE 380 marks the first time undergraduate students from the University of Louisville Department of Industrial &amp; Systems Engineering had a poster accepted at HFES.
      </p>
      <ul class="session-details">
        <li><strong>Location:</strong> Riverside East</li>
        <li><strong>Date and Time:</strong> Wednesday, October 15, 5:30-6:30 PM CDT</li>
      </ul>
    </section>
  </div>

    <p>
      The aggressive driving systematic review is led by my Ph.D. student Yan Chen, who is doing an incredible job connecting human factors and AI-driven modeling to real-world transportation systems.
